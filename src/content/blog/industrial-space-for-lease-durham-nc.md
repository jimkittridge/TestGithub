Durham's industrial market is tight. The Triangle added 14,000 jobs in recent years, and Research Triangle Park (RTP) keeps pulling demand outward. If you're looking for industrial space in Durham, you're competing in a market where vacancy is under 5% and good locations move fast.

Here's what you need to know about pricing, where to look, and what a lease actually covers.

## What You're Paying: Pricing Broken Down by Location

Industrial rents in Durham vary by corridor. Base rent ranges from $14 to $22 per square foot annually, depending on location and building class. Add NNN charges (property taxes, insurance, CAM) on top of that, and you're looking at total occupancy costs of $17 to $25/SF all-in.

South Durham properties near I-40 rent around $18 to $22/SF base. These are newer flex buildings with good truck access and easy interstate connectivity. East Durham warehouses run cheaper, $14 to $18/SF, because the buildings are older and farther from the main corridors. RTP and Meridian Parkway command premiums, $20 to $25/SF, because tenants are paying for proximity to major employers and newer construction.

Let's put that in real dollars. A 1,500-square-foot unit at $20/SF base rent plus $4/SF NNN runs $2,700 per month in total occupancy cost. Most leases you'll see are NNN, which means you pay the base rent to the landlord and your share of property taxes, insurance, and common area maintenance to a third party.

## The Main Neighborhoods

Durham's industrial footprint breaks into distinct submarkets, each with different characteristics.

**South Durham (I-40 Corridor)**
This is where the action is. South Durham has the most new construction and tenant movement. You're looking at 14 to 20 feet of clear height, 10 to 14-foot drive-in doors, and reliable 100 to 200 amp electrical service. Tenants here are a mix of contractors, light manufacturing, e-commerce operations, and service businesses that need quick I-40 access. Rents: $18 to $22/SF. Move-in time is usually 2 to 4 weeks because most buildings are move-in ready.

**East Durham**
Older inventory, lower rents. Buildings here were built 10 to 30 years ago and tend to have less clear height and smaller doors. But rents are $14 to $18/SF, and if your equipment needs aren't extreme, you save real money. East Durham is experiencing revitalization as contractors and small manufacturers move in and upgrade buildings. It's a value play if you don't need the newest structure.

**RTP and Meridian Parkway**
This is premium territory. The Research Triangle Park submarket attracts tech-adjacent tenants, professional services, and light industrial operations that benefit from being near corporate parks. Clear heights run 14 to 20 feet, electrical is modern, and buildings are typically newer. Rents: $20 to $25/SF. These are move-in-ready spaces, and landlords expect professional tenants with strong credit.

**North Durham (I-85 Corridor)**
Oriented more toward distribution and logistics. I-85 access makes sense for companies moving goods north/south. Buildings tend to be larger, rents are competitive at $16 to $20/SF, and you'll find more 3,000 to 5,000 SF options here. It's less about small flex space and more about regional logistics.

## What a Lease Actually Covers

When you lease industrial space in Durham, you're signing an NNN lease in almost all cases. Here's what that means.

Base rent covers the landlord's cost and profit. In Durham flex space, that's typically $18 to $22/SF annually at South Durham locations. You pay that regardless of how hard business is going.

NNN charges are estimated annually and billed monthly. Property taxes on a 1,500-SF unit in South Durham might be $3,000 to $4,000 per year, or roughly $2 to $2.50/SF. Insurance (landlord's coverage, which you reimburse) runs $0.50 to $1.50/SF depending on building condition and use type. CAM, or common area maintenance, covers parking lot, lighting, trash, and landscaping; it's usually $1 to $2/SF in a flex building.

So if you're in a 1,500-SF unit at $20/SF base rent, plus $4/SF NNN, you're budgeting $3,600 to $3,750 per month. Some leases cap NNN growth at 2 to 5% annually, which protects you from sudden surprises.

## Lease Terms You'll Encounter

Lease documents are long, but a few items matter most. Permitted use should clearly include your business type. If you're a contractor running a shop, make sure the lease allows "contractor operations" or "light manufacturing" or whatever fits. Some landlords restrict certain trades for noise or emissions.

Alterations and improvements: Ask what you can modify without landlord approval. Drive-in door installations, electrical upgrades, flooring, and racking need to be spelled out. Some landlords will pay for structural improvements; others won't.

Operating hours and after-hours access: Most modern flex buildings offer 24/7 access for an additional fee, $50 to $150/month depending on building. If you need late-night or early-morning access, negotiate this upfront.

Parking for work trucks: Confirm how many parking spots you get for crew vehicles and whether work trucks can remain on-site overnight. This matters if you run a crew-based operation.

Assignment and subletting: If your business changes or you need to relocate, can you lease the space to someone else? Most leases allow this but require landlord approval. Know the rules before you're stuck.

## Moving Fast in Durham

Industrial space in Durham moves. Good locations in South Durham, especially units with drive-in doors and solid electrical service, lease within 30 to 60 days of listing. If you find a space that fits, have your lease ready to sign within two weeks. Landlords want move-in as fast as possible, and tenants who can demonstrate financial strength and signing readiness get deals done.

Before you commit to any location, spend time in the building during business hours. Talk to existing tenants. Check the parking and dock loading areas in person. Internet quality, water access, and after-hours security are real considerations that don't show up on a lease abstract.

## FAQ

**What's the typical lease length in Durham industrial?**
Most leases are three to five years, but one-year and two-year terms are available, especially in flex buildings. Some landlords offer month-to-month after your initial term expires.

**Can I modify the space or add electrical service?**
Yes, but you need landlord approval in writing. Structural changes and electrical upgrades are usually permitted with proper permits. Get this in writing before you start work.

**What's the difference between South Durham and East Durham pricing?**
South Durham near I-40 rents $18 to $22/SF because it's newer and has better truck access. East Durham runs $14 to $18/SF because the buildings are older and farther from major corridors. Both markets work; it depends on your priorities.
