You're starting a contracting business or expanding your operation. You've got tools, equipment, and maybe a work vehicle. Someone suggests a storage unit. It's cheap, it's available, and you can lock it up at night. Sounds reasonable. Then you actually try to run your business out of it.

Storage units and contractor bays solve different problems. Here's the real cost and operational comparison.

## The Storage Unit Math

A standard 10x20 storage unit in Durham costs about $141 per month. That's 200 square feet for $141, or $0.71 per square foot per month. Over a year, that's $8.52/SF. It looks cheap on paper.

Here's what you get: a lockable box. No power. No drive-in or roll-up door access (you unload by hand). No climate control. No workspace to do anything. Typical hours are 6 AM to 6 PM or 7 AM to 7 PM, so if you need after-hours access, you're paying extra for a gate code or key.

That $141/month gets your stuff out of the weather. If you're storing seasonal equipment, old machinery, or personal items, it works fine. But if you're running a business out of it, the constraints pile up immediately.

## The Contractor Bay Reality

A contractor bay in the Durham area, let's say 950 square feet with drive-in access, costs around $20/SF NNN. That's $1,583 per month for base rent plus NNN charges. Total occupancy cost is closer to $1,750 to $1,900 per month depending on exact location and lease terms.

Here's what you actually get: a space where you can run your business. Drive-in door, so you can park your work truck inside. 14 to 20 feet of clear height, meaning you can set up racking, hang equipment, or position a lift. 100 to 200 amps of electrical service, so you can run a compressor, welder, or power tools without worrying about blowing a breaker. 24/7 access. Separate HVAC so your tools aren't stored in the heat or cold.

You can meet clients there. Your crew can stage equipment and materials. You can run a legitimate business with a legitimate address.

## When Storage Makes Sense

Use a storage unit for actual storage: seasonal equipment you're not touching for months, archived documents, old inventory that might sell someday, personal items you need to keep secure. If you're using it 2 to 4 times a year, it's the right tool.

Don't use it as your daily operational base.

## When a Contractor Bay Makes Sense

You need a contractor bay if you answer yes to any of these:

You have work vehicle that needs to be parked somewhere. Parking it on the street gets old fast, and clients don't love seeing three work trucks in a residential neighborhood.

You need to stage materials and tools. If you're constantly running to the garage for supplies, you lose productivity and money to logistics.

Your crew meets there before or after jobs. A 200-SF storage unit can't accommodate three people and a layout table.

You run any equipment requiring significant amperage. A compressor, welder, or heavy-duty drill press needs real electrical service, not a standard outlet.

You want to look professional. A business address with a real facility tells clients you're legitimate. A P.O. box doesn't.

You need after-hours access. Most storage facilities lock at 6 or 7 PM. Contractors often work early and late.

## The Lease Structure

A contractor bay comes with an industrial lease, typically one to five years depending on the building. You pay base rent plus NNN charges. Most landlords ask for first month's rent, last month's rent, and a security deposit. Standard terms are three to five years, but shorter terms are available in flex space.

You'll sign an NNN lease, meaning you reimburse your share of property taxes, insurance, and common area maintenance. At $20/SF base rent in South Durham, NNN typically adds $4 to $5/SF, so your all-in cost is $24 to $25/SF annually.

Can you negotiate the lease? Yes. First-time lessees often get a small rent concession or tenant improvement allowance if they're willing to commit to a three-year term. If business is slow or the building has high vacancy, you have leverage.

## The Real Financial Picture

Let's run a 12-month cost comparison for a contractor who has a work truck, needs to park it somewhere, and wants to stage equipment and materials.

Storage unit: $141/month times 12 = $1,692 per year. Add a second 10x10 unit because the 10x20 is full and doesn't have drive-in access: $62 times 12 = $744. You're up to $2,436 annually for two units totaling 300 square feet with no drive-in, no electrical, no workspace.

Contractor bay: $1,800 per month times 12 = $21,600 per year. That sounds like a lot. But you get 950 square feet with drive-in access, electrical service, 24/7 access, and a professional workspace. Cost per square foot is $22.74/SF annually, which is actually cheaper than renting storage space and a separate commercial office.

More importantly, you save labor hours. You're not loading and unloading by hand. Your crew isn't wasting time fetching tools from three locations. You can meet clients at the facility instead of apologizing about your home setup.

The bay pays for itself through operational efficiency.

## FAQ

**Can I run a business out of a storage unit?**
Technically, yes. Practically, no. Most storage unit leases prohibit commercial operations, and the space doesn't support it. No power, no drive-in access, and limited hours make it inefficient. A contractor bay is the right infrastructure for a working business.

**What if I only need 300-400 square feet?**
Contractor bays typically start at 950 square feet, so you'll be oversized. But you'll outgrow 300 square feet quickly once you add a second employee or more equipment. Start with the space you need in two years, not today.

**Can I negotiate the contractor bay price?**
Yes. Shorter lease terms (one year vs. three years) cost more per month. Longer terms get you discounts. Strong credit and references help. In soft markets, landlords offer concessions. In tight markets (like Durham), you take what's available.

**What if I need 24/7 access but the bay doesn't offer it?**
Most flex buildings offer 24/7 access for $50 to $150 per month. Ask before you lease. It's a standard upgrade.
