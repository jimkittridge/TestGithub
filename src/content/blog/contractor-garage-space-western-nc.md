Contractor work is booming in Western NC. The region's construction cycle, post-Helene rebuild, and tourism infrastructure keep general contractors, plumbers, electricians, HVAC crews, and heavy equipment teams busy. That means contractor garage space is in high demand and short supply.

The problem is simple: purpose-built contractor bays are scarce. Most contractors are renting flex space designed for light industrial use or converting old retail into makeshift work bays. Neither option is ideal.

## Why Contractor Space Is Hard to Find

Asheville's industrial vacancy is 2.9%. That's extremely tight. Contractors need specific things: drive-in or roll-up doors, 200 amp electrical (not 100), clear floor for equipment storage, high enough clear height for tall tools or vehicles, and parking for multiple trucks and vans.

Most inventory in Fletcher and Arden is generic flex space. It has one overhead door, maybe 150 amps, and was designed for warehousing or light manufacturing, not for contractors running a full operation. You can make it work, but you're compromising.

Brevard and Hendersonville have older industrial stock from the '80s and '90s. Cheaper rent, more space, but building condition varies widely and electrical capacity is often an issue.

## What's Actually Available

[Get Flex Space](/) operates small warehouse bays in Fletcher, near I-26 and Airport Road. These are 1,000 to 3,000 SF units with 14 to 20 feet of clear height, 100 to 200 amp electrical, drive-in doors, and 24/7 access. You can lease for 1 to 5 years and move in as fast as 2 weeks.

These work for contractors who need a base to stage equipment, store materials, and park crew vehicles overnight. You can outfit one with a work bench, tool storage, a small office, and a coffee maker.

Beyond Get Flex Space, you've got smaller industrial parks scattered in Fletcher and Arden with similar specs but less flexibility on lease terms. You've got converted retail spaces, some of which have roll-up doors cut into the front; these are cheaper but usually lack the electrical and parking you need. And you've got older dedicated shop buildings in Brevard and Hendersonville, if you're willing to trade location for lower rent.

## Cost Breakdown

Base rent (NNN) for contractor-suitable flex space in Fletcher runs $18 to $22 per square foot annually. NNN comes to another $400 to $600 per month on top of base rent, depending on building age and operating expenses.

Here's a real scenario: a 2,000 SF bay in Fletcher at $20 NNN costs $33,000 annually in base rent, plus about $5,000 to $7,000 annually in NNN. Total monthly is $3,200 to $3,400.

Older stock in Brevard or Hendersonville might be $12 to $16 NNN, which saves you $3,000 to $4,000 a year. But you're 45 to 60 minutes from most Asheville jobsites, and the building might need electrical upgrades if you're adding a third power tool.

## The Helene Factor

Hurricane Helene hit in September 2024. Reconstruction is creating urgent demand for contractor staging space, equipment storage, and temporary crew bases. This demand is pushing rent up and vacancy lower. If you're working on rebuild projects, you need space now.

The rebuild cycle probably runs through 2026 and into 2027. That means contractor demand will stay high, which means rents won't drop and availability won't improve much. If you're a contractor, lock in a 3 or 5 year lease now while units are available.

## What to Look For

Electrical is non-negotiable. If you're running table saws, compressors, welders, or other high-load equipment, you need 200 amps. Check the panel. Ask about voltage. A 100 amp panel in an older building might have limited capacity for additions.

Drive-in or roll-up doors matter if you're loading trucks daily or moving equipment in and out. A man door (standard 3x7 foot entrance) isn't enough. You need something you can back a 20 foot trailer through.

Clear height determines how you can use the space. 14 feet is a minimum. 16 feet is comfortable. 20 feet gives you options for storage racks or tall machinery. Asheville has older stock at 12 feet; it constrains everything.

Parking is underrated. You need space for your truck, crew vans, and client vehicles. Older buildings sometimes have street parking only. That won't work if you've got a crew of five.

Floor condition matters. Concrete with cracks or settling will trash your equipment over time. New concrete is better, but resurfaced concrete is fine. Buckled or soft concrete is a red flag.

## Location Trade-Offs

Fletcher offers the best combination: newer space, I-26 access, good electrical, and parking. It's the premium market. Arden is 10 minutes south; rent is slightly lower and landlords are more flexible. Hendersonville is 30 minutes south; rent drops another $3 to $4 per square foot, but you're commuting a long way.

If you're working in Asheville and west Asheville (most of the tourism and downtown work), Fletcher keeps you close. If you're working in Brevard or the south county, Hendersonville is smarter.

## Lease Negotiation

Landlords in a 2.9% vacancy market have leverage. But they also want reliable tenants who'll stay 3 to 5 years. Use that. Propose a 3 year lease in exchange for reduced rent or free rent for the first 30 days. Ask about tenant improvement allowances. Some landlords will cover paint, minor electrical upgrades, or concrete patching.

Get everything in writing: utilities you pay vs. landlord pays, responsibility for roof and exterior, whether you can add signage (important for contractor visibility), and your right to sublet or assign.

## FAQ

**Q: Do I really need a drive-in door?**
A: If you're loading trucks daily or moving equipment in and out, yes. If you're mostly office-based and occasionally storing materials, a man door might work. A standard 3x7 door isn't big enough for pallets, equipment, or trailers. Budget for a drive-in if you can.

**Q: What if the building only has 100 amp service?**
A: You can run most tools on 100 amps if you stagger usage and don't run everything simultaneously. But a utility upgrade (to 200 amps) costs $3,000 to $8,000 and takes 6 to 8 weeks. Avoid this headache. Ask for 200 amps before you sign.

**Q: Is Hendersonville far enough to save money?**
A: You save $200 to $300 monthly on rent, but you lose time on every commute. If you're driving 45 minutes to an Asheville jobsite, you're burning $10 to $15 in fuel and lost time. The math only works if most of your work is in south county or Brevard.
