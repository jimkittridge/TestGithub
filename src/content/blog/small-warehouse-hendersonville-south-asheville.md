Hendersonville is 30 minutes south of Asheville and most contractors know it's out there. What they don't always realize is that Hendersonville has warehouse space at lower rent than Fletcher, with less competition, and still decent I-26 access. If you're working in south county or Brevard, Hendersonville makes more sense than a long commute from north Asheville.

## The Hendersonville Advantage

Rent is $2 to $4 per square foot lower than Fletcher. That's not tiny. On 2,000 SF, you save $3,000 to $4,000 per year.

Vacancy is higher than Fletcher (maybe 4 to 5 percent vs. 2 percent in Fletcher). That means more options, better negotiating leverage, and landlords more willing to cut deals.

I-26 runs through Henderson County, giving you highway access north to Asheville and Greenville, and south toward Hendersonville proper. It's not as tight as Fletcher's I-26 access, but it's still the major corridor.

The drawback: commute. Hendersonville is 30 to 45 minutes from central Asheville. If 70 percent of your work is in Asheville, Hendersonville is a daily time sink. If 70 percent of your work is in south county (Brevard, Flat Rock, Horse Shoe), Hendersonville is perfect.

## What's Available

Hendersonville has older industrial stock from the 1970s through 1990s, plus some newer flex space built in the 2000s. Building quality is mixed. Some buildings are well-maintained. Others are neglected.

Most units are 1,000 to 3,000 SF, similar to Fletcher. Clear height runs 12 to 16 feet (less than Fletcher's 16 to 20). Electrical varies; some older buildings have 100 amp, newer stock has 150 to 200.

Drive-in doors are common but not universal. Hendersonville landlords are more flexible on customization, so if a building doesn't have a drive-in and you need one, you might negotiate adding one as part of the lease.

## When Hendersonville Makes Sense

**South county contractors**: Plumbers, electricians, HVAC in Brevard, Horse Shoe, Flat Rock, and Transylvania County. Your jobsites are 15 to 30 minutes from Hendersonville. Your commute to Asheville is 30 to 45 minutes and not worth it. Hendersonville is home.

**Distributors serving south**: Parts suppliers, tool rental, materials distribution serving contractors south of Asheville. Hendersonville is on the main corridor. Asheville proper is out of the way.

**Retail and service businesses not client-facing**: If clients don't visit your space, commute doesn't matter. You're staging operations and handling logistics. Rent savings are real; commute is irrelevant.

**Manufacturers and light assembly**: Woodworking, metal fab, appliance repair, equipment builders not tethered to jobsites. Rent savings matter. Asheville location doesn't matter.

## When Fletcher Is Better

**Central Asheville contractors**: General contractors, commercial trades working Asheville proper and west Asheville. Fletcher is 15 to 20 minutes. Hendersonville is 40 to 60 minutes. The extra commute kills profit.

**Service businesses meeting clients**: Consulting, business services, any operation where clients visit your space. Location signals. Asheville-based clients prefer Asheville. Fletcher over Hendersonville.

**Urgent response requirements**: HVAC emergency in January, you need to get a truck to the caller fast. Fletcher is your base. Hendersonville delays you by 30 minutes.

## Commute Math

Hendersonville to Asheville: 30 to 45 minutes depending on traffic and exact location. South Asheville (near Fletcher) is closer; north Asheville or downtown is further.

Hendersonville to Brevard: 20 to 30 minutes.

Hendersonville to Greenville, SC: 60 to 75 minutes via I-26 south.

A contractor in Hendersonville working 70 percent in Asheville and 30 percent in south county is spending 7 to 9 hours per week commuting from a Hendersonville base. Over a year, that's 350 to 450 hours, or roughly 9 to 11 weeks of full-time commute. If you bill hourly, that's real loss.

But if that contractor's work is 70 percent in south county and 30 percent in Asheville, Hendersonville base saves the same time.

## Building Quality in Hendersonville

Hendersonville's older stock is hit-or-miss. Some buildings from the 1980s have been well-maintained by owners who care. Others are neglected, with roof leaks, electrical issues, and aging HVAC.

Ask to see maintenance records. Walk the space carefully. Check the roof (in sunlight, if possible). Look at the electrical panel; how old is it? Ask about recent repairs.

Newer Hendersonville flex (2000s and later) is more reliable. You know what you're getting. But it costs more.

The rent savings in Hendersonville sometimes come from accepting older buildings. That's fine if you've inspected and you know what you're buying.

## Utilities and Operating Costs

Utilities in Hendersonville are similar to Asheville; maybe slightly cheaper due to less mountain wind and slightly lower heating demand.

Budget $200 to $400 monthly for electric on 2,000 SF, depending on equipment and climate.

Water is usually tenant responsibility; budget $50 to $100 per month if you're using water at all.

## When to Sign a Lease

Hendersonville's slightly higher vacancy means you have some breathing room. You can afford to wait for better terms. Don't take the first unit offered.

Negotiate: propose 3 year lease for 10 percent off base rent. Ask about tenant improvements. If the building is older, propose the landlord handle a roof section repair or electrical upgrade in exchange for a signed 3 year lease.

Hendersonville landlords want longer-term occupants; they have less leverage than Fletcher landlords. Use that.

## Highway Alternatives

If Hendersonville feels too far, consider Arden. Arden is 10 to 15 minutes further south of Fletcher, but still only 20 to 25 minutes from central Asheville. Rent is slightly less than Fletcher ($18 to $20 vs. $20 to $22) and inventory is often better. The split between Fletcher and Arden is often the right move for Asheville-based contractors.

## FAQ

**Q: Is the Hendersonville commute really that bad?**
A: It depends on your work location. If you're based in Hendersonville and 70 percent of your jobsites are in Brevard or south county, commute is minimal. If you're based in Hendersonville and 70 percent of work is Asheville downtown, you're losing time and fuel. Do the math on your specific jobsites before you commit.

**Q: Can I negotiate rent in Hendersonville?**
A: Yes. Vacancy is slightly higher than Fletcher, and landlords are less aggressive. Propose a 3 year lease at 5 to 10 percent off quoted rent. Ask for tenant improvements. Many Hendersonville landlords will negotiate to get a reliable long-term tenant.

**Q: What if a Hendersonville building needs electrical work?**
A: Ask the landlord to handle it as part of your lease negotiation. If the building only has 100 amp service and you need 200, propose the landlord cover the upgrade cost (usually $3,000 to $8,000) and you'll sign a 5 year lease. It's worth asking.
